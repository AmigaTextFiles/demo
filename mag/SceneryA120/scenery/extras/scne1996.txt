At The Gathering, new supergroup TBL did what everyone expected them to do
sooner or later: make a truly great demo! With power-members like Danny,
Louie, Facet, SuperNao and Rodney, they were pretty much destined for
greatness, winning not only the demo competition but the 4k intro and
graphics competitions as well. At the same party, Norwegian group
Triumph reached 2nd place with (to my knowledge) the first demo to directly
support 24 bit graphics cards - "Dreamscape".

On the PC side of things, ex-amiga people also dominated. Demos like CNCD's
"Inside" (TG winner) and Oxygene's "Contrast" all featured the work of ex-
Amiga sceners, and brought a new sense of design and quality to the pc demo
scene. For the first time, pc demos could really rival those of the Amiga.

In other alternative platform news, it's worth noting that the c64 showed
some promise this year, with several of the larger parties hosting c64
competitions with good participation. It was not until the end of next year,
however, that we would see the revolutionary participation at The Party 7!


The groundbreaking game "Tomb Raider" was released by Core Design this year,
and its credits roster contains several interesting names: Lead coding by
Paul Douglas (Dan/Lemon.?), additional coding Gavin Rummery, Jason Gosling,
additional psx coding Del Leigh-Gilchrist (Del/Anarchy), Dude Howe. Lead
artist/animator Toby Gard, artists Neal Boyd, Heather Gibson.

17-19.01  Oz '96.
-----------------
OZ96 was held in NSW, Australia, and it was a small (only 59 official
visitors + some who didn't bother paying :) party for the oz PC scene.
Entries were shown on a 486DX-2/66mhz instead of the promised DX-4/100mhz,
but there were few complaints. Entries from contestants not present at the
party were allowed, but only the ones present could receive prizes. Goblin's
4k intro crashed when it was shown, and was disqualified. This party has
been called Ozone 96 on some occasions (even by me! =D), but the true name
is/was always just Oz (which is slang for Australia, if you didn't know).

the third Saturne party, the first in two years, and the first to support

submitted. Another Saturne party, Saturne IV, was held in November.

justice in this world. People like Flan, Lowlife, Bez and not least Slaine
delivered excellent pieces of work, yet the people voted for inferior
material.

---------------------------------------------
ICI96 was held at Vättlehallen in Stenkullen near Gothenburg, Sweden by C-
Lous, Sardonyx (amiga), Yodel and Goto 10 (pc). About 350 people attended;
most from the western parts of Sweden, but a few Norwegians also showed up.

were a few problems with the delivery of power, and as usual, the party
network went down a few times, but nothing really out of the ordinary =) A
few local bands played music on the stage, an arrangement the organizers had
to put up with in order to rent the hall. Reportedly, most of them should
have stayed in their garages and practized for a few more years... ;) There
was no separate raytracing competition, so several traced pictures competed
in the graphics competition. Official t-shirts (designed by Duffe/Sardonyx)
were sold at the party.

50mhz with an unspecified amount of memory. Demos must be max 5mb in size.
The pc compo machine was an AMD 486DX4-120mhz with 8mb RAM. Most popular
soundcards were available. Competitions were taped to s-video before being
shown on the bigscreen.

results! There was also a fast intro competition.

now defunct. Instead, take a look at http://www.remedy.dcs-graphics.se for

27-29.09  Skenery 1996.
-----------------------
SKE96 was held in Palokka, Jyväskylä, in central Finland by by Zymosis,
Bombsquad and Da Jormas. About 260 people attended. Against all common
trends, this party attempted to recreate some of the atmosphere of the
early copy parties...and succeeded! First of all, noone complained if you
had a beer or eight. The party developed into a giant party, with drunken
people everywhere. The Police came several times, and in the end busted up
the party, which by then had become a giant Rave Party. A Skenery'97 is
being planned...
  Demo and intro competitions were held mixed, with Amiga and PC
contributions competing against each other. The amiga machine configuration
was 060-50 with 16mb fast, but the production had to run on 030-50 8mb.
PC configuration was P75 16mb, 1MB S3, GUS or SB16/SBPRO. Productions had
to run on 486DX2 with 8mb. Wunderbaum's "He Loves You" (amiga) was not
shown, due to a broken disk or something...
