Scene Strike 1 Review

So, it finaly happened. I came home, a fresh '7' in my index, Physics off my mind, and I can sit down and type away about my memories of Scene Strike 1... Here goes;

The odd thing about people is that they never read the invitations to stuff they attend. We had clearly wrote that the partyplace opens up at 12:00, that the deadline is 18:00 and the projections start at 20:00, but people don't seem to have noticed that.

At first, some of them showed up at noon, got all surprised to see that the "party didn't begin" (?!) and left, only to return at six, AFTER the bloody deadline, to turn in their work. Next time, people, READ THE FUCKING INVITATION! Okay, now, that's enough about that.

Although for most people the party, as I said up there, started at either noon or six o'clock, for us it began three days ago. But I already wrote a CTW review, so I'll skip those three days, and hop on over to the morning of Scene Strike. The only thing I remeber doing, after the clock buzzed at 09:00 sharp, was busting the poor thing to pieces and somehow, automagicaly, appearing at Rex an hour later. Amazing, aren't I? I am still wondering where did I loose that hour. Was it, perhaps, one of those _abduction_ things? "The truth is out there..."

Anyway, there wasn't much work there for most of us. Dominator, Dule the Bauk, Q, Arhimed and M-G were quite busy, but most of us, the _others_, just bummed around, helping out from time to time. So, I said to myself, it's about bloody time you finished up your goddamn demo. I sat down by the "Motherfucker" and started typing away. I typed for quite a while, and had all the procedures ready, when I noticed the guys mentioned above; The People Who Didn't Read the Invitation. I didn't pay much attention to them, since I was in desperate search of axs to one of the pentiums lying around useless, to be able to stick all my procedures into one program and time the whole thing to the music. Someone has taken away the key. That someone was M-G. And he was nowhere to be found.

So, what the fuck, I sat back by the machine and made another procedure or two, by the time we were supposed to lay out the chairs... oh, yeah, people were starting to arrive. I gave up on the coding for a while, to greet all the attendants and bypassers I knew... and there were quite a few of them...

Zordon somehow managed to appear, but only briefly, as well as Norge, Dragon, and most of the oldskool fuckarounds from Ecstasy. The Hex people were there, and they brought a couple of machines along, one of them being a C64, which has an entire paragraph of it's own a bit lower in the article. Then there were the Hyperopia/Juice people, who also have a paragraph or two of theirs, though not for quite the same reasons as the Hex guys... Phew... then there were most of the people from the yu-scena mailing list, most of MindEscape, Simargl, better known as "Panta the Amazing", along with Sony, and quite a lot of other groups, whom, unfortunately, I didn't get to meet, or have forgotten in all the chaos and darkness of C-Rex.

I started doing my code again, by this time it was around 17:00, and got nowhere, because M-G appeared, but he had to do some stuff with his pentium, so I was once again stopped in finishing the demo on time. No big deal, though, because the deadline got moved because of the BLOODY PEOPLE WHO DON'T READ INVITATIONS I mentioned a few paragraphs above. Anyway, my girl arrived, so I was a bit busy entertaining her, her sister, Nck, and a few other people who were trying to help my (lousy) coding with their (even lousier) advice.

Paranoid hung around, and finding he had nothing to do, he wisely concluded that he could collect entries for the Amiga on his machine. So, still wisely, he entered DPaint, made a logo saying "ENTRIES FOR THE PARTY - HERE!", then, since it looked too smallish, he doubled the whole thing, and thus produced one of the more entertaining entries in the pixel art compo.

After a while, M-G finished his work by the pentium, and I finaly got axs to it, only to find out that my modplay routines don't work on dos 7.0... FUCK, FUCK, FUCK!!! I tried like hell to fix 'em up, but when, waaaaay past six thirty, it was obvious I couldn't do it, I just gave up, and pissed as I was, created a 64k .exe which blanked the screen and whistled. That was my intro compo entry, and the story behind it. I packed all the other stuff I had, and gave my entries over, some to ParanoiD, some to Dominator, depending on the machine they were supposed to be run on. So, that about covers the boring "what Phuzzy did for the compos" bit, and I can now get over to the actual party report.

As I said, the deadline got delayed more and more, because most people just DON'T READ INVITATIONS, and then, consecutively, the projections were delayed as well, and most of the entries weren't tested. This produced a couple of unwanted results. But, let's take it one bit at a time.

Quite a while after eight o'clock, the projections finaly begun. First were the Multichannel compos, which were... well... quite boring... Hazma of Titan won, with his "Human Existance" track, and second and third were, of course, BP and DJ Dot. Pop, or what? ParanoiD did well, though, with my absolute favourite of them all, SuperNindzaOsvetnik (SuperNinjaAvenger)... Not much to say, except that the insane Hazma dude made, if I recall things right, a 15Mb module (?!) with loads of guitar samples... another module refused to run under Cubic Player, which made for a bit of screech-scratch I enjoyed a lot, but most of the audience was deeply disturbed. Whatever...

Right after this, my girlfriend had to leave, so I had to walk her and Bepa up to the tram station. Next in line were the 4-channel modules, my fav cathegory, so I strongly believe I beat the "running down Jevrejska street through rain" all-time speed record. But, I got back in time.

Most of the tunes were sort of hardcore, and although well done, they strongly lacked originality. So, all the "not-hc" tunes were up at the top of the list. Haralampie, a motherfucking INSANE kickass cool dude, won, of course, with his oriental-hindu-singading-hare- lampije-swami tune... I was, of course, second, with the best module of them all (modest, aren't I? ;-) and next were Kozmo, DJ Nick (of course... pop, or what?) and a LOUSY tune I thought sucked big time. But whatever. The thing that surprised me is that Alex didn't do better, because his mod was, by my oppinion, near perfection. Technicaly excellent, with a gr8 idea and everything... but... what the hell... life goes on, or somehing...

Ok, now, FUCK me if I can recall the order in which the compos followed, so I'll just take them at random... the 64k intro featured a boring, but nicely coded intro from Mad Virgin (a cube, shaded, mapped, and all sorts of other chilly-willy stuff being done to it), an intro by Nevermind which, of course, won, because at the time it seemed like an original and great idea, although the code was even beneath my standards... and, as it turned out, the idea was ripped, too, from some other party (basicly, they had a "texturemapped" dot)... if I had known this at the time, they would certainly not have gotten my vote. Third was, of course, my intro called hardcode, for obvious reasons, and it amazed me by actualy getting VOTES! Hehehhe...

The demo compo was completely dominated (dominatored?) by Branimir and Tosha, Hyperopia's coders... although it didn't have the best gfx, nor the best sfx, the code was abso-fuckin-lutely-amazing, so their "washing" got the well deserved first place. Pas Maters, on the other hand, got the also well deserved second spot, with great pixels, a funky tune, and an excellent message... Corrosion had great pixels and a great tune, no message, and the code... well, I think it is sufficient if I say that we have installed a mechanism in Suffocator's and Griffin's Amigas that detects any unauthorised movement away from the screen and sends 20,000 volts up their bottoms. So, as you may guess, they are working hard on improving their technique . A well deserved third place.

The wild compo was a tough one. Three entries, all of them animations, two of them kickass productions. Hive studio won, and they deserved it, although I prefered the CoRRoSioN promo anim. Both were complex, well designed, excellently timed, and plain fuckin' wonderful. The third one, in my oppinion, sucked, with no sound and not much gfx, either, but that is probably only because it was facing such mighty opponents.

Soooo, now we come down to the pixels... Jamon, of course, won (pop, or what?) and pain following close by (do I have to repeat the "pop, or what?" anymore?)... nice pictures, I am not a pixelator, so I can't judge them, but I found no original idea in them. Alex found a few bugs on the pictures, but if you want to discuss that, ask him about it. I know zilch. Although that didn't stop me from entering my own pixelation of an Alien... which looks kind of a bit like the Riddley Scott "Alien"... only mine looks a bit different... and has only two colors... and has only contours... and not much of a contour... oh, well... back to the masters at work... nope's flower pot man, a beautiful autoportait, was third, although I like it best, and Alex was fourth. Paranoid, unfortunately, only snitched ninth place, with his aforementioned "APPLICATIONS" pix... pitty, that bitmap font was realy something.

The photoshop compo, or the "effects allowed" compo, didn't have many entries, and I won't comment it much, again, because I am not a gfx artist, so I know dick about what I am saying. Fodder won, and Vita was second, although for the life of me, I couldn't see why wasn't Vitomir's picture disqualified. Don't get me wrong, it was funny and everything, but among three beautiful DRAWN pictures, a simple snapshot of a TV screen kind of seems out-of-place. Hive studio was only fifth, even though they had the only true artwork in the competition. Even my resistance, created in about 15 minutes of screwing around with "smooth" in DP got placed better. Something stinks there.

The trace cathegory was OK, although, again, a "piece of cake" work won. Konzerva (Can) by Eagle is a simple "Whiskas" texture wrapped around a cylindrical object. Whereas the second spot, vitomir's proba19, was a complex mirror-balls object traced in his very own tracing routine... the others were nice, but no cigar for them this time.

Last, but not least, the ascii compo. Remember that I told you how the Hex dudes get an entire paragraph of their own? Well, here it is. Abyss won the ascii compo with his ABSOLUTELY AMAZING piece of asc2 art made on his C64!!! He brought it along, we hooked it up to the video beam, and by George, he did it! The pictures weren't much to look at, but the sole fact that he made them on his C64 were enough for him to win.

The other pictures weren't much to look at, technicaly great, most without an idea, except for green tooth's which had an idea, but no technique, and mastermind's, which had neither. Mine I will not comment, because it was just a piece of shit I threw together in 30 seconds after I realised that my demo will now be finished and that I still didn't have an ascii compo entry.

Phew, that about covers the compos. Now, let's talk a little bit about attitudes. Here comes the paragraph I said would be dedicated to the Hyperopia/Juice people. I don't know if it was jealousy, hatred, or simply idiotism on behalf of some (notice; some, not all) members of the aforementioned groups, but whatever it was, it was plain stupid to shout out smartass remarks about some of the CoRRoSioN entries, and not only CRS stuff, but also anything that didn't meet up with what they thought was "good". BULLSHIT!

If they realy are the _artists_ they consider themselves to be, they should know what kind of an effort goes into creating any tiny bit of a splotch on the screen, or even the lousiest of bleeps through the speakers. If they know that, then it is plain shitty manners that allow them to behave as they did. Grow up. We can bullshit about each other on boards, fuck each other's moms in productions, but when we get together to liven up our limp scene, I think a little collegial attitude is expected and welcome. If you hate something, don't vote for it. It is as simple as that. If you _realy_ hate that something, tell everybody sitting beside you not to vote for it. Actualy, you can go around the room telling people not to vote for it. But shouting out stupid "supposed-to-be-funny" comments about others' efforts, is a major assholish thing to do. Think about all this. I hope none of that will happen again at Scene Strike 2. Because if it does... well, you should know that we keep Alex heavily armed and feed him only once a week. He is _hungry_ for blood. Oh, yeah, just so that I don't seem biased against the H/J people; this doesn't go only for them. Others were noticed doing this, too, but not as intensely as they did. What I said goes for all of you. Think about it, and stop acting like immature children.

After the projections were finished, with only three mistakes by the guys in the control room, one of them being them not playing Zordon's module - for which I humbly appologise to Zordon right here and now, on behalf of the entire organising crew, we collected the vote sheets and started the long and strenuous counting. There were "only" 91 sheets, with three votes per cathegory, but trust me, that is a whole LOT of counting to do. And the vote count proggy wasn't finished in time. So, six of us insane people locked ourselves in a room with a bottle of Coke and counted away, while the crowd outside got more and more nervous... fortunately, noone broke in the door and stoned us to death, and after quite some eyesight lost, we were done, the results were out, Dominator, ParanoiD and myself announced them, and handed over the prizes to the winners. I must notice that Nope Dope was the only cool dude amongst them, he was the only one that gave me a sweet little warm hug after the "strong and manly handshake" we exchanged. By this time, most of the people packed their stuff and left, but we stuck around and screwed around with the remaining chosen few... then, at early morning, we packed up as much stuff as we could, crammed ourselves into two cars, and went out to, finaly, after a starved out night, get some food into ourselves. So we did, and the part of SS1 that stuck in my brain hardest, was the entire CoRRoSioN posse munching franticaly at bits of pizza and sipping coke at the "Orao"... oh, yeah, and reading chosen quotes from one of the books we were supposed to give away as prizes, but were too ashamed to do so... "Programming in Clipper" bullshit... I snitched one, just in case... money makes the world go around, you know? After that, we split up, each of us heading home to grab lots of sleep to be as fresh as possible (which means; stoned motherfuckers) for the concert the next evening. You can read all about it in the next article in here, "Damage 2", by yours truly...

Okay, that about covers everything. Okay, so it doesn't cover everything. But I have no more NRG left in me, I just passed a goddamn exam, and I am finishing this (already too long) article right here, wishing to see all of you, with a shitload of productions at Scene Strike 2, which will, most likely, be held in September, this year (1997)... I wish you all a good night and sweet dreams!

Phuzzy Logik of CoRRoSioN
