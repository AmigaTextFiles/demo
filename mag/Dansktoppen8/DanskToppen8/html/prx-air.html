Interview

By PuRyX/Unique

Uhm... sorry sir.. do we know you?

Hoi, I'm Airwalk the Polkabrother.
[pRx] -> PuRyX/Unique
[Air] -> Airwalk/Polkabrothers

[pRx]: Hmmm... Polka Brothers I've heard that name before ;) - when did they start, and what is your history in PB?

[Air]: Uh, I'm so bad at numbers.. Let's see, we must have started around 93. Vandrup (former Easley/Parasite) and I formed Polkabrothers, and ran it as some kind of joke for a while, still being members of our groups at that time, Parasite and Kefrens.

[pRx]: We've seen some Polka Brothers around lately (you, Slide, Pixie (now known as Toft)), all with their amiga's - does that mean we can expect some kind of eagerly awaited release from PB soon?

[Air]: "Soon" is relative. I cannot promise any release at some special time at all - but I think IMHO that one day there'll be something comming from us again, as we still run Polka as a Hoby. I can say that Slide is working, Pixie is working as hell, and I'm coding. But it's been like that for quite a while now :)

[pRx]: What does an oldtime scener work with in real life - in other words what do you do for a living theese days?

[Air]: I study engineering, programming and electronics.

[pRx]: When was the last time you got up too late, but just stayed in bed anyway? :)

[Air]: Haha, yesterday :)

[pRx]: Is the Amigascene the same now as it was when Polka Brothers ruled? And what is the difference?

[Air]: "When Polka ruled" - my God, it was never ment to be like that.. I see stunning demos from everywhere nowadays, from people I've not even heard of - maybe because I haven't made much contacts since that time. One difference seems to be the interest in hardware rather than software. As Merge once joked, "Who needs Fastmem - Only people that can't code". Let's go A500 again, see where C64 went - and they're still working!

[pRx]: What do you think is real quality in a demo/intro?

[Air]: Music (which a miss these days - BOM BOM BOM BOM ..) and design. Coding is not even nessasary.

[pRx]: Did any releases so far match that definition? :) - in other words, mention some of your favourite Amiga demos/intros and tell us why they're your favourites.

[Air]: DCS's KLONE makes me come.. (Not that the code is bad :)) This guy Adam really knows how to find the right colors :)

[pRx]: Oh! I just love that demo aswell!

[pRx]: Did you know that it's possible to push a cow so that it falls on the ground while it's asleep?

[Air]: Yes, I tried it - and I still have that headache now and then :)

[pRx]: Uh oh.. now here's a hard one! - Will you keep your amiga AND your amigascene spirit?

[Air]: Sure, I have 4 amigas. The spirit changed, but so did the scene..

[pRx]: Oh yeah.. btw: What is THE Amiga spirit in your eyes?

[Air]: Nowadays amigaspirit is far to much interested in HW, rather than SW. Be creative with what you got!

[pRx]: What would you do RIGHT NOW, if you were free to do anything you wanted?

[Air]: Eat lunch, have sex and goto sleep ;)

[pRx]: This IS a real coder! He does a 'goto sleep'. Wonder where that label is... ;)

[pRx]: Which was the best party you ever visited?

[Air]: Gathering 93, Gathering 94, Symposium 99.. Galemand meetings :)

[pRx]: Has your time running around like a weirdo ;) in the amigascene given you any kind of usefull experience, that you'll be able to use in 'real life'?

[Air]: Sure, I'm using that every day. Learning computer technics through programming demos is ALOT more fun than through stupid school "Hello World" projects!

[Air]: Yes, sure. Anyway I'm not particular interested in peoples HW configs - it's a sucking expencive hobby - start programming!
