Changes to this Issue of The Word





  The  most noticeable change which you can all see is the new real-time
clock/timer  which  is placed at the top right corner of the screen.  It
was one of the recommended improvements in Dave Diamond's article (Issue
13).  I did state that this could be done which it has.

  Its  quite  a useful feature that gives some attention and indications
to  the reader of how long you've been reading an article or running the
disk  mag and so on.  I've been testing it recently of how long it takes
to  make  my  tea, talking on telephone, pop to the shop and back etc...
Anyway it works.


How the clock was done
======================

   The clock was first written as a separate program before merging with
the  main  Word  source.   The main bulk of the work was the integration
stage where the clock needed to synchronise with the entire program.  It
didn't  seem simply but fortunately I've already inserted a routine just
so the clock can operate independantly to the rest of the program.  Even
so  in  the  next stages I'll be looking into making sure that the clock
operates more effectively without too much program interference.


About the clock itself
======================

  The  clock  ranges  from 00:00:00 through to 23:59:59 before reverting
back  to  its  initial  state.   In  addition - if you press <R> on your
keyboard  at anytime during The Word - this will reset the clock back to
00:00:00.   This  extra  function is added to the info.  file on "How to
use The Word".


Why isn't the clock visible during the Preferences?
===================================================

  I feel it was only necessary to display the clock during the main menu
and  whilst  you're in "viewing artcle" mode.  There was no need for the
clock to show up in the prefs.  unless you really want me to I could...
	

Clock Accuracy
==============

  If  you  place  a  wrist  watch or a real clock against the one in The
Word,  you  will  find  that  the internal clock will tick about a split
second  faster  for  every  second  it  increments.   But  this  is very
unnoticeable.   I  will  at  some point make attempts to fine tuning the
clock.   But  so far I'm very satisfied with its current tick speed.  It
doesn't  make  a  big difference to the timescale we're currently using.
At least the World is not connected to this clock! Or may be?

  Well  theres  little  else to mention about the clock.  Otherwise just
sit back and watch it tick!
