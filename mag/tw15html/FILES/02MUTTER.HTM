Why is it that people only seem to tell you stuff when it's far too late
for you to do anything about it?  Almost everyone must have at some time
bought something, only to be told "You could have got it cheaper at such
and  such".   Why?   Why do some people take such a perverse interest in
upsetting people in this manner?

Another more recent one was about my scene days on the Amiga, I got some
E-Mail  basically saying "oh yeah, you were dead popular, everyone knows
you"  and  so  on, fat lot of good it is telling me this AFTER I've quit
the  bloody  scene  innit?   If people had actually said something to me
while  I  was  thinking of getting the PC then I might not have bothered
and I might just have kept my old A1200.

I wonder if a lot of this is because people are a bit timid where making
suggestions  is  concerned?  Sometimes if you're up to something, people
do  not  like  to say anything in case you consider them nosey, and tell
them  where  to  get off.  In most situations a little bit of thought is
all  you  need  to  decide  whether to speak up or not, if you have some
information and you think it would be useful, tell someone!

Of  course, it would help if the person doing the buying, programming or
whatever  made sure that other people around them actually knew what was
going on!  That way there would be a lot less of this sort of thing!  Us
lads  up  here in Lincolnshire *always* discuss stuff first so that this
sort  of  thing  doesn't  usually  happen,  if  someone  is going to buy
something for their computer, car, office or home or whatever, they make
sure that other people know about it, just in case!

NFA  were like this at one point, if we had not gone halves with Carnage
when we did then The Word could well have gone under when myself, Phill,
WB  and  several others all left NFA.  As it is we were able to pass the
work  on to Kei of Carnage (who probably still hates me for it :) who in
turn could use his better web and Amiga connections to spread it more.

However,  The  Word  now  seems to be suffering another article drought:
Gone are Aladdin Sane, Pex, John, Deck the Ripper, Budda, DF0:  and Elf,
gone are all the people who sent in the odd advert and article, gone are
all  those  donations  of  text  and  graphics  from  unknowns and Amiga
regulars.

If you look at it now The Word is filled with articles from the same few
people  every issue, Myself, Carnage, bootblock of Terraform, Andy Bolt,
Dave  Diamond, Mammoth, and er...  is that it?  Where are the legions of
workers  who  took  it to it's peak, when Fish/LSD called it "The number
one  Amiga  magazine"?   They've  deserted  us,  thus we need more fresh
talent from all over the place.

The internet might actually help with this, Kei tells me that users from
a  couple of European countries have pledged their support, and the HTML
version  might bring articles in from the PC side of things, so if these
things  take  off  then we'll have the support we need to keep it going.
Unfortnately,  this will take the magazine away from what it once was, a
magazine  for  the Amiga UK scene who don't want to be up to their necks
in  pure  100%  scene rantings.  It will be a shame, because that's what
made The Word so cool, but if it survives, maybe it'll be worth it.

More rantings in the next issue, maybe, who knows?  :)
