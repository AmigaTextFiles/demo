About  a  year ago I did some reasearch into dates and time measurement.
Not  counting  the  fact that we do not exactly have the exact date that
this  planet  was  created  or  man first started living on it, over the
centuries  there  have existed many methods of telling the time/date.  A
day is measured in revolutions of the planet around the sun (ie one) and
there  are some who say it has gotten shorter over the centuries.  Also,
BC  (Before Christ) there were many civilizations with their own ways of
telling the date and only one was chosen, then inverted to work out when

untill  we  have  the  average  year  we  have now based on astronomical
factors  with  error  compensators  such  as  leap years (whether 2000AD
should  be  a  leap  year is still being discussed by astronomers to yet
again compensate for a possible error).
 So  with  all this it is probable that we are not telling time right at
